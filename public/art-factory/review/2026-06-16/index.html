Current Aesthetic Read

The current direction is Quiet Scenic Classics: minimal literary landscapes and architectural thresholds on pale paper / marble-white underlays. The Art Deco and character-heavy scene explorations are now superseded.

  • Use Penguin/Oxford-like canonical seriousness for motif selection, without copying their layouts or branding.
  • Build covers around scenery, horizons, buildings, roads, seas, gates, windows, moons, stars, and one symbolic clue.
  • Keep people absent or tiny; use them only as scale marks, with no detailed faces or dramatic bodies.
  • Use 3-5 large flat shapes, one warm accent, and strong blank title-safe space.
  • Keep covers textless for Tome, with large calm negative space for mobile readability.
  • Use pale paper / marble-white / warm ivory underlay instead of cream parchment dominance.
  • Use bright disciplined accents and avoid beige drift, purple cast, and dark monotone palettes.
  • Avoid vulgarity, grotesque imagery, gore, monsters, Art Deco, photorealism, 3D, ornate frames, and fake typography.
  • Use Stoa backgrounds as left-weighted symbolic worlds with large quiet UI zones.
  • Use sheets 25-28 as the current reference family, then make individual scenic finalists and map approved assets into Tome book records.
