# Meridian Signal Covers Template

> Status: current production direction for Tome app front-cover generation.
> Keep it flat, minimalist, light, white-marble based, and restrained. Do not
> drift into photorealism, painterly scenes, dramatic lighting, or extra
> narrative detail.

## Source Read

This direction combines two proven sources:

- Recent Meridian Classics white-underlay covers: warm ivory field, restrained classics seriousness, flat symbolic image, limited colors, simple icon/scene logic, and strong repeatability across hundreds of books.
- Tome batches 26-28: title-safe blank upper fields, calm roads/gates/windows/water, small symbolic accents, and safe genre tuning without gore, grotesque imagery, or crowded character scenes.

The result should not copy Meridian trade dress. It should translate the same discipline into textless Tome app cover art.

## Aesthetic Name

Meridian Signal Covers

## Core Idea

A Tome cover is a quiet signal, not a full illustration. Each book gets one calm place or icon, one symbolic clue, and one controlled color accent. The cover should imply the story with literary innuendo: a key near a gate, a red ribbon on an empty chair, a lit upper window, a lone road, a sail at moonrise, a rose beside a covered frame.

The strongest proof is `35-meridian-signal-covers-white-underlay-v2.png`. It is closer to recent Meridian than the painterly `34` proof because it uses flatter vignettes, simpler icon panels, and more warm blank field.

## Layout

- Canvas: vertical 2:3.
- Ground: full warm ivory / white-stone underlay.
- Upper field: 28-35% mostly blank for Tome title UI.
- Image zone: centered middle/lower symbolic vignette or simple lower panel.
- Optional footer: a muted color slab or narrow band, with no text.
- Composition: one icon/place shape, one path/water/road/window/light cue, one symbolic object.
- Element count: 3-5 large forms.
- Figures: absent by default; tiny scale marks only when the book needs them.
- Texture: subtle paper grain, matte screenprint softness, no photoreal or painterly finish.

## Palette

Use color, but keep it disciplined:

- Warm ivory: `#f3ead7`
- Stone white: `#f7f2e8`
- Deep teal: `#1f6f73`
- Soft sky: `#9fc9d8`
- Pine green: `#3f6a52`
- Dusk navy: `#20334c`
- Pale gold: `#d8a24a`
- Terracotta/coral: `#d9784d`
- Sage: `#8aa579`
- Fog gray: `#b9beb6`

Avoid purple drift, beige-only covers, neon color, and dark monotone palettes.

## Symbol Grammar

Build each cover from three signals:

- Setting signal: garden, road, shore, bridge, room, gate, tower, field, harbor, window, stair, house, civic arch.
- Object signal: key, ribbon, lantern, sail, rose, empty chair, rope, mirror, lily, letter-shaped cloth without readable letters.
- Light signal: moon, sun disk, window glow, lamp, reflected water, red signal, star, fire ember.

The object should be suggestive rather than literal. It should feel like a clue discovered by a reader.

## Do

- Keep the upper title field quiet and usable.
- Make the icon or panel readable at thumbnail size.
- Prefer calm scenery over figures.
- Use one warm accent per cover.
- Let the book-specific meaning come from placement and absence.
- Use Penguin/Oxford-like seriousness for motif choice without copying their layouts.

## Do Not

- No readable text, fake title, fake author, letters, or logos inside the art.
- No Art Deco, cubism, photorealism, 3D, ornate frames, or busy collage.
- No detailed faces, dramatic bodies, gore, monsters, skulls, vulgarity, or grotesque imagery.
- No crowded streets, many props, dense decorative foliage, or overly specific scene reenactments.

## Prompt Template

```text
Create a textless Tome app book cover in the Meridian Signal Covers style.

Canvas: vertical 2:3. Use a warm ivory / white-stone ground across the whole cover. Keep the top 28-35% mostly blank for future title UI. The middle/lower area contains one simple symbolic vignette or lower panel.

Book: [TITLE]
Concept: [ONE SENTENCE ABOUT THE SYMBOLIC READING]
Scene: [ONE PLACE: road, room, garden, harbor, gate, shore, field, tower, window]
Symbolic clue: [ONE OBJECT: key, ribbon, lantern, rose, sail, empty chair, rope, mirror, lily, etc.]
Light cue: [moon, window glow, sun disk, lamp, reflected water, star, ember]

Style: refined modern literary cover illustration; flat editorial screenprint texture; subtle paper grain; 3-5 broad shapes; quiet but colorful; recent Meridian white-underlay discipline; modern classics seriousness.
Palette: warm ivory, stone white, muted teal, soft sky blue, pine green, dusk navy, pale gold, terracotta/coral, sage.
Figures: absent or tiny scale marks only; no detailed faces.

Avoid: readable text, letters, logos, fake typography, cubism, Art Deco, photorealism, 3D, ornate frames, busy props, crowded scenes, gore, grotesque imagery, monsters, skulls, vulgarity.
```

## Example Motifs

- `The Turn of the Screw`: pale house, garden gate, lit upper window, small key at the threshold.
- `Billy Budd`: moonlit sea, sail triangle, coil of rope, lantern glow.
- `The House of Mirth`: dusk garden, dark water, white lily, one warm townhouse window.
- `Main Street`: prairie town street, water tower, broad orange sky, single lit window.
- `The Return of the Native`: open heath, winding path, low moon, small bonfire ember.
